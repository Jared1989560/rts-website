Why Independent QAQC Matters

Independent quality assurance isn't a formality — it's the last line of defense before a missed item becomes a commissioning delay, a safety event, or a restart. Rogue brings an outside set of eyes that has no stake in rushing the punch list closed.

We verify that E&I installations match specifications, P&IDs, and project documentation before loop checks begin — catching issues at the point where they're cheap to fix, not after energization.

Inspection Scope

  • Instrument installation verification against data sheets and P&IDs
  • Cable tray, conduit, and wiring installation checks
  • Grounding and bonding continuity inspection
  • Junction box and marshalling panel verification
  • Control panel and MCC internal inspection
  • Hazardous area equipment certification review (NEC Class/Division, ATEX)
  • Punch list generation with photo documentation
  • Pre-commissioning readiness assessments

Documentation Deliverables

  • Inspection reports tied to tag numbers and line items
  • Photographic punch list packages
  • Pre-commissioning completion certificates
  • Redline markup of as-built discrepancies
  • Non-conformance reports (NCRs) with corrective action tracking
  • Final QC release documentation for turnover

Who This Is For

  • EPC contractors needing independent third-party QC sign-off
  • Owner operators who want verification before accepting turnover
  • Project managers whose internal QC team is stretched thin
  • Insurance or lender requirements for independent inspection
  • Facilities preparing for regulatory audits or safety reviews

Standards & Codes

  • NEC (NFPA 70) installation requirements
  • NFPA 72 fire alarm and detection
  • API 670 machinery protection instrumentation
  • ISA-5.1 instrumentation tagging and identification
  • IEC 61511 / ISA 84 safety instrumented systems
  • Client-specific inspection test plans (ITPs)
